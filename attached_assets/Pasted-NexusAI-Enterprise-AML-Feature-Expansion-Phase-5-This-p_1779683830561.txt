NexusAI: Enterprise AML Feature Expansion (Phase 5)
This plan outlines the next iteration of NexusAI, targeting the specific requirements of tier-1 financial institutions and banks. Based on our brute audit of the platform, we have identified key missing features that are standard in modern AML/Fraud software.

User Review Required
IMPORTANT

Please review the proposed features below. Are there any specific modules you want to prioritize first? Should we focus on the Customer Risk Profile (XAI) or the Regulatory Reporting Hub?

Proposed Changes
We propose building four new core workflows to bridge the gap between our current platform and enterprise industry standards.

1. Customer Risk Profile & Explainable AI (XAI)
Currently, risk scores are static numbers. Regulators demand transparency ("Explainable AI").

[NEW] 
RiskProfile.tsx
Create a comprehensive dynamic dashboard for individual customers/entities.
Implement an XAI component that breaks down the math behind a risk score (e.g., Base Score: 20 + Geographic Risk: 15 + PEP Match: 40 = 75).
Include historical risk trend charts.
2. Adverse Media & Ongoing Screening
AML isn't just about transactions; it's about external data.

[NEW] 
WatchlistScreening.tsx
Build a real-time feed interface for scanning adverse media (news articles).
Interface for resolving false positives from PEP (Politically Exposed Persons) and Sanctions list matches.
3. Ultimate Beneficial Owner (UBO) Discovery
Banks need to know exactly who owns the businesses they bank.

[NEW] 
UboDiscovery.tsx
A specialized workflow for corporate unwrapping.
Visual breakdown of holding companies to identify natural persons with >25% ownership.
4. Regulatory Reporting Hub
SARs are currently approved but not sent anywhere.

[NEW] 
RegulatoryReporting.tsx
An integration dashboard to monitor the status of e-filed SARs to FinCEN / FINTRAC.
XML/JSON export previews for batch reporting.
