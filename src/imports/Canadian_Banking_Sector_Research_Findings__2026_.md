# Canadian Banking Sector Research Findings (2026)

## 1. Common Problems Across the Big Five (RBC, TD, BMO, Scotiabank, CIBC)

### Macroeconomic and Geopolitical Pressures
- **Trade Uncertainty and Tariffs:** Lingering uncertainty regarding trade policies and potential tariffs is creating an unfavorable operating environment, particularly for banks with significant U.S. exposure (like TD and BMO) [1, 2].
- **Higher Credit Costs:** Banks are facing rising credit costs and loan loss provisions as consumer leverage remains elevated and the economic outlook stays cautious [3, 4].
- **Geopolitical Tensions:** Global instability is impacting market volatility and investor sentiment, affecting wealth management and capital markets segments.

### Operational and Digital Challenges
- **Digital UX Gap:** While mobile banking is central, Canadian banks have historically lagged behind global peers in areas like card controls, self-service tools, and seamless digital onboarding [5].
- **Legacy System Constraints:** Many incumbent banks still struggle with fragmented data and legacy infrastructure, hindering their ability to deploy AI and other advanced technologies rapidly.
- **Fraud and Security:** Sophisticated fraud tactics are weighing on consumer confidence. 7% of Canadians considering switching banks cite dissatisfaction with how their bank responded to a fraud incident [5].

### Strategic and Competitive Hurdles
- **Stalled Neobank Momentum:** Momentum for digital-only banks has stalled, with many being owned by incumbents (e.g., Tangerine by Scotiabank, Simplii by CIBC), leading to evolutionary rather than revolutionary change [5].
- **AI Trust Gap:** 94% of Canadian consumers have concerns about using AI with their bank, primarily due to privacy and data security fears. Only 36% are comfortable with automated financial advice [5].
- **Asset-Light Growth Needs:** Regulatory pressures and capital requirements are pushing banks to find ways to grow revenue without significantly increasing their balance sheets.

## 2. Market and Customer Research Insights

### Customer Sentiment
- **Cautious AI Adoption:** Canadians are among the most cautious globally regarding AI in finance. They prefer a "blended model" that combines AI efficiency with human support [5].
- **Value-Added Expectations:** There is a growing demand for rewards, benefits, and information services without incremental fees.
- **Switching Triggers:** Beyond fraud response, customers are driven to switch by better pricing, superior digital experiences, and more personalized advisory capabilities.

### Market Trends
- **Embedded Finance Opportunity:** There is a significant opportunity for banks to provide infrastructure for non-financial companies, driving fee-based revenue.
- **Wealth Growth Potential:** Despite economic headwinds, there is a strong focus on capturing a larger share of the investment wallet as consumers seek better returns and personalized advice.
- **Focus on Inclusion and Transparency:** Banks are increasingly investing in accessibility tools and transparent communication to improve perceived value.

## 3. Market and Customer Research Deep Dive

### Market Share and Dominance
- **The Big Six Dominance:** RBC, TD, Scotiabank, BMO, CIBC, and National Bank dominate the Canadian market, holding approximately **89.5% of all banking assets** [6].
- **RBC at the Top:** Royal Bank of Canada remains the largest, with over **CA$1.57 trillion in assets** and significant profit growth in 2025 [7, 8].

### Consumer Behavior and Demographics
- **Cautious Optimism:** Consumers enter 2026 with cautious optimism, balancing improved financial sentiment with lingering uncertainty from elevated borrowing costs [9, 10].
- **Generational Variances:** Younger generations (Gen Z and Millennials) are more open to digital-only players and AI-driven tools, while older demographics remain loyal to incumbents but demand better digital interfaces [11].
- **Fragmentation Revolution:** A "customer fragmentation revolution" is occurring, where consumers increasingly use multiple financial providers for different needs (e.g., one for daily banking, another for investments, another for high-interest savings) [12].

### Open Banking / Consumer-Driven Banking (CDB)
- **Legislative Progress:** The *Consumer-Driven Banking Act* (CDBA) and its 2025 updates are moving Canada toward a formal open banking framework [13].
- **Phase 1 (Read-Only):** Currently underway, allowing secure data sharing via APIs, eliminating the need for risky "screen scraping" [13].
- **Phase 2 (Write Access):** Expected by mid-2027, enabling actions like payment initiation and account switching directly through third-party apps [13].
- **Consumer Empowerment:** CDB aims to give consumers a comprehensive view of their finances and access to tailored products, but trust in data security remains a major hurdle [13].

### Key Feature Demands
- **Real-Time Infrastructure:** Demand for instant payments and real-time transaction visibility.
- **Advanced Card Controls:** Features like instant card locking, spending limits, and virtual cards are becoming priority areas for catch-up [5].
- **Personalized Advisory:** Consumers want AI to provide predictive insights and behavioral analysis, but with clear human oversight [5].
